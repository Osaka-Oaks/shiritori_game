しりとりとは? What is Shiritori?

Shiritori (しりとり) is a traditional Japanese word game. The word literally means "taking the end." You take the last sound of your opponent's word and use it to start your own!

assignment

Basic Rules 基本ルール

1

Players take turns saying a word.
交代で単語を言います。

2

Must use Nouns only.
名詞のみ使えます。

3

Start your word with the last kana of the previous word.
前の単語の最後の音から始めます。

4

No repeating words!
一度出た単語は使えません。

stars

Pro Tips & Edge Cases 特殊なルール

chevron_right Small Kana (ぁ, ぃ, ぅ...)

You can use either the whole syllable or just the small kana. Example: Kaisha (かいしゃ) ends in Ya.

chevron_right Long Vowels (ー)

In Katakana, use the vowel sound before the dash. Example: Takushii (タクシー) ends in i.
