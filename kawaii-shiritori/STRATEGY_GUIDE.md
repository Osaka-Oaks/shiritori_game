# 🎯 Shiritori Strategy Guide for Native Speaker Games

## 🎮 Goal: Stay Alive Longer

When playing with native Japanese speakers, **your first goal isn't to win—it's to stay in the game longer** and build your vocabulary through real practice.

---

## 📋 Quick Shiritori Rules

1. Say a word that **starts with the last kana** of the previous word
2. **Don't use words ending in ん (n)** — automatic loss
3. **No repeating words** already used in the game
4. Use **nouns only** (no verbs, adjectives, particles)

---

## 🎯 Strategy #2: Force Easy Kana

**Try to end your words with these sounds:**

### Safe Ending Sounds (Many Follow-ups)

- **か** → かさ, かばん, かめ, かお, かぜ, かみ
- **さ** → さかな, さくら, さる, さとう
- **た** → たまご, たいよう, たけ, たぬき
- **な** → なつ, なまえ, なす, なべ
- **は** → はな, はし, はこ, はる
- **ま** → まど, まち, まつり, まくら
- **ら** → らっぱ, らくだ

### Example Strategy Chain

```
ねこ (neko) → ends in こ
  ↓
You know many こ words:
  こども (child)
  こえ (voice)
  こおり (ice)
  こころ (heart)
```

---

## ⚠️ Strategy #3: Avoid Dangerous Endings

### Fatal Ending (NEVER!)

- **ん** - Instant automatic loss!
  - ❌ らいおん (raion - lion)
  - ❌ みかん (mikan - orange)
  - ❌ ぱん (pan - bread)
  - ❌ ごはん (gohan - rice)

### Difficult Endings (Limited Options)

- **ゆ** - Very few words start with ゆ
- **ぢ** - Rare starting sound
- **づ** - Rare starting sound
- **ぴ** - Limited vocabulary
- **ぎ** - Harder for beginners
- **を** - Almost no words start with を

## 🧠 Strategy #5: Practice by Category

Native speakers often think in categories. Train yourself to do the same!

### Quick Category Recall

**If you hear: ね**
Think: Animals → ねこ (cat), ねずみ (mouse)

**If you hear: か**
Think: Objects → かさ (umbrella), かばん (bag), かめ (turtle)

**If you hear: り**
Think: Food → りんご (apple)
Think: Animals → りす (squirrel)

**If you hear: た**
Think: Food → たまご (egg)
Think: Animals → たぬき (raccoon dog)

## 📖 Strategy #6: Learn Hiragana, Not Romaji

Native speakers think in hiragana:

**Think like this:**

```
ねこ → こども → もも ✅
```

**NOT like this:**

```
neko → kodomo → momo ❌
```

Your reaction speed improves dramatically when you **visualize the hiragana** instead of transliterating through romaji.

## 🏋️ Best Way to Practice Alone

### Daily 15-Minute Routine

**Day 1-7: Flashcard Drill**

- Pick a kana (e.g., か)
- Write 10 words starting with it
- Say them out loud with Japanese pronunciation
- Check translations

**Day 8-14: Chain Building**
Chain words together and watch for traps:

```
いぬ (inu - dog)
  ↓
ぬの (nuno - cloth)
  ↓
のり (nori - seaweed)
  ↓
りんご (ringo - apple)
  ↓
ごはん ❌ (gohan - rice)
TRAP! Ends in ん!
```

**Day 15-30: Speed Practice**

- Set 30-second timer
- Generate words for random kana
- Use practice mode in the app
- Track your streak

## 🎯 Long-Term Goal

**Know 5 words for every hiragana character** = ~230-250 words

At this level, you'll be **surprisingly competitive** with casual native speakers and can survive longer in actual games.

---

## 🎓 8-Week Training Program

### Week 1-2: Foundation (50 words)

- Focus: あ-お (vowel sounds)
- Practice: Flashcards 15 min/day
- Goal: Instant recall of 5 words per sound

### Week 3-4: Common Sounds (50 words)

- Focus: か-こ, さ-そ
- Practice: Chain building
- Goal: Link words smoothly

### Week 5-6: Extended Vocab (50 words)

- Focus: た-と, な-の, は-ほ
- Practice: Speed drills
- Goal: <3 second response time

### Week 7-8: Competition Ready (80 words)

- Focus: ま-も, や-よ, ら-わ
- Practice: Mock games vs bots
- Goal: 5+ turn survival

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Before the Game

1. ✅ Review your weak sounds
2. ✅ Warm up with 5-minute practice
3. ✅ Remind yourself: goal is survival, not winning
4. ✅ Relax and have fun!

### During the Game

1. ✅ Listen carefully to ending sound
2. ✅ Think category first (animal? food?)
3. ✅ Check if your word ends in ん before saying it
4. ✅ Don't rush—accuracy over speed
5. ✅ Learn from opponent's words

### After the Game

1. ✅ Write down new words you heard
2. ✅ Add them to your practice list
3. ✅ Note which sounds gave you trouble
4. ✅ Practice those specifically

---

## 🌟 Advanced Tactics

### Defensive Play

If you're struggling, **end with safe sounds like か, さ, た** to give yourself easier options next turn.

### Offensive Play

If you want to challenge opponent, **end with rare sounds like ゆ, ぴ, ぎ** (but be ready for them to know words you don't!).

### Reading Opponent

- If they hesitate on certain sounds, force those
- If they're strong on animals, try objects/places
- Match their pace to reduce pressure
